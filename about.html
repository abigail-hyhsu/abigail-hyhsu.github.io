About Me

I was born and raised in Taiwan, where I earned a BS in Applied Mathematics from National Chung Hsing University in 2020. I completed an MS in Mathematical Sciences at National Chengchi University in 2024, where, under the supervision of Shuenn-Jyi Sheu and Tzuu-Shuh Chiang, I studied stochastic analysis and large deviation theory, investigated the large deviation principle of regime-switching diffusion processes, and completed my master’s thesis on this topic. From 2024 to 2025, I was a research trainee at the Institute of Mathematics, Academia Sinica, where I worked with Ching Wei Ho on functional analysis.

In Fall 2025, I began my PhD studies in Mathematics at the University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee under the supervision of Chao Zhu. My current research focuses primarily on stochastic analysis and its applications, including regime-switching diffusions, coupling methods, ergodicity, and the long-time behavior of stochastic processes. I am also interested in random matrix theory and its applications.

Outside of mathematics, I enjoy reading books on psychology and philosophy and reflecting on the meaning of life. Some books I especially recommend are How to Know a Person by David Brooks, The Cafe on the Edge of the World by John Strelecky, Tuesdays with Morrie by Mitch Albom, Compassion in the Court by Frank Caprio, and When Breath Becomes Air by Paul Kalanithi.
